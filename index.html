Introduction to Cyber/Data Security

"The process of verifying a user or device before allowing access to a system or resources." From StrongDM

Authentication allows you to make sure that it is only you going into your programs or software, no one else. This could be used, for example, with multi-factor authentication for accounts you own. These require more than one way to verify your identity.

"A process by which a server determines if the client has permission to use a resource or access a file." From Boston University Tech Web

Authorization makes sure that you or someone you trust are the only people accessing files or programs that belong to you. This can be used with permission requests via text and/or emails.

"A form of data security in which information is converted to ciphertext." From Fortinet

Encryption ensures that your files and data are coded to prevent hackers from accessing and reading your information and documents. Codes can scramble the original message so that if your data is accessed by a hacker in any way, so that the hacker can't decipher or figure out the text.

Social Media


  • Phishing is deception that relies on impersonation and fakery. Social media is free to use and open to all. This means fake profiles are shockingly easy to create. Since social media platforms are all slightly different, attackers have developed niche tactics specific to each site to help evade detection.

  • A spam bot is a computer program that helps to spread spam across the Internet. Spam bots often scrape contact information, create fake user accounts, or operate stolen social media accounts

  • In 2020, 32% of phishing attacks used social media as a vector. By 2021, the global cost of cybercrime damage was expected to reach $6 trillion annually

    • How to Stay Safe on Social Media

  • "If you did not initiate the communication, you should not provide any information." from occ.gov
    Never give your personal identification to strangers or anyone on the internet.

  • Be extremely careful of messages or emails that require an urgent request. Many times these attacks create a false sense of urgency and prompt you to react to protect against some "virus"
